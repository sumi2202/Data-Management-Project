How can I eat better?

Be Consistent and Stick to the 4 Food Groups

It's important to have a meal that has portions from all four of the food groups. Nothing tops the importance of water, so make sure you have a glass on the side of your plate.

What Happens When You Eat Unhealthy?

Your Body Reflects What You Feed It

Junk food contains a lot of carbohydrates, sugars, and starches. While having a bag of chips as a quick snack is alright every now and then, in the long run it is detrimental to your health.

Why Do We Need All 4 Food Groups?

A Healthy Plate Comes From Balance

They say too much of a good thing can eventually turn into a bad thing. Sure, veggies are important. However, only eating veggies will have you missing out on the nutrients that only protein items, grains, or dairy could provide.

Easy Shrimp Pappardelle

Ingredients

  • 5 tablespoons unsalted butter, divided
  • 8 garlic cloves, sliced
  • 1 lb. large 20-30 ct. shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/4-1/2 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes
  • 8.8 oz. egg pappardelle
  • 1/2-1 cup starchy pasta water

Instructions

  1. Fill a medium pot with water. Bring to a boil and season with salt. It should taste as salty as the sea. Because we're only using 1/2 lb of pasta, you do not need to fill a HUGE pot with water, you want the water to be starchy since we're using it in the sauce. I like for the water to only come up about 1/2-inch over the pasta.
  2. Heat a large skillet to a medium heat. Add 4 tablespoons butter. Once butter melts, add garlic. Saute for 2 minutes. Be careful not to burn.
  3. While the garlic cooks, season the shrimp on both sides with salt and pepper. Add shrimp to the skillet with the butter and garlic. At the same time, add the pasta to the boiling water. Cook shrimp until bright pink and curled up, about 3-4 minutes. Cook pasta until aldente.
  4. Once the shrimp are done, if the pasta isn't cooked yet, remove from heat. (You don't want them to overcook. When the pasta is done, add to the shrimp, garlic, and butter, return to the heat but reduce it to low. Add red pepper flakes and 1/2 cup of pasta water. Toss to combine, and then add remaining 1 tablespoon of butter, toss again. If needed, add more pasta water to create desired consistency (I added another 1/2 cup). Season pasta with salt and pepper.

5-Ingredient Peanut Noodles

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp. sesame oil, divided
  • 16 oz. bag coleslaw mix (cabbage, carrots, red cabbage)
  • 1/4 tsp. kosher salt
  • 3/4 lb. thick spaghetti
  • 1/3 cup hoisin sauce
  • 1/4 cup creamy peanut butter
  • /4 cup + 1/8 cup water

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Season liberally with salt.
  2. Heat a large skillet to a medium heat. Add two teaspoons of oil. Once the oil is hot, add the coleslaw mix and 1/4 tsp salt. Cook until soft, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. While the veggies cook, add pasta to water. Cook until aldente. Reserve cooking liquid.
  4. While the pasta cooks, add hoisin, peanut butter, water and remaining sesame oil to a medium bowl. Whisk to combine.
  5. Once the noodles are cooked, use tongs to transfer them to the veggies. Add sauce and toss to combine. If necessary, add a little bit of pasta water to loosen the sauce up. Season to taste with salt and pepper. Serve hot, room temperature or cold.

5-Ingredient BBQ Chicken-Stuffed Sweet Potatoes

Ingredients

  • 1 large sweet potato
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1 rotisserie chicken breast
  • 1 cup red cabbage(100g), shredded
  • ⅓ cup barbecue sauce(85 g), plus more for serving
  • 2 tablespoons green onions, thinly sliced

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a small baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Using a sharp knife, carve out one of the breasts from the rotisserie chicken. If the chicken is particularly small, use both breasts. Chop the chicken breast into very small pieces and set aside.
  3. Use a fork to poke holes all over the sweet potato. Transfer the sweet potato to the prepared baking sheet.
  4. Bake for 40-50 minutes, until the sweet potato is tender when pierced with a paring knife or skewer.
  5. Add the cabbage to a medium skillet over medium heat. Add the barbecue sauce and sauté until the cabbage starts to soften, about 3 minutes.
  6. Add the chopped chicken and stir just to incorporate. Remove the pan from the heat.
  7. Once the sweet potato is cool enough to handle, slice it in half lengthwise and scoop out the flesh with a spoon until a ¼-inch (6 mm) layer remains.
  8. Fill the sweet potato skins with the chicken and cabbage filling, drizzle with barbecue sauce, and top with the sliced green onions.
