What Is The Key To Better Sleep?

Ever Heard of The Circadian Rhythm?

The Circadian Rhythm is what regulates your body's sleep-wake cycle. It's actually a part of your brain that responds to the brightness of your environment. Tuning into YOUR Rhythm will help you have a better understanding of how your sleep cycle works.

Sleeping Late Affects Your Rhythm

This Actualy Deteriorates Your Overall Health

The older we get, the harder it seems to go to bed early. However, it is especially important that we get a full 8 Hours of sleep. Sleeping late shortens your body's sleep schedule and your brain automatically wires itself to adapt to less hours of sleep. This will eventually lead to insomnia and other sleeping disorders.

How Can One Fix This Issue?

There Are Many Ways to Properly Reset Your Sleep Schedule

To start, try shifting your bedtime up by 15 to 30 minutes. It may take a few days or weeks for your body to adapt, but you will get there! Also, it is important that you avoid any blue light(usually from your phone or computer screens), and create a proper sleeping environment for yourself.

Cozy Up In Bed With These Tips

Insomnia

A Brief Overview

  • Around 1 in 3 people have mild insomnia sometimes
  • Many poor sleepers have develooped poor sleep habits
  • Insomnia can be treated with cognitive behavioural therapy
  • Taking sleeping pills is NOT effective
  • To avoid sleeping disorders, such as Insomnia, try to sleep early

Creating The Perfect Sleep Environment

Set Your Mind Into The Zone

  • Dilute lavender oil and water and spray light mists in the corners of your room
  • Keep the room temperature as cool as possible
  • Keeping the room as dark as possible helps regulate your circadian rhythm
  • Reduce the noise level. Turn on Do Not Disturb
  • Drink a warm cup of milk or water

Meditation Tips

A Peaceful Mind Brings Good Dreams

  • Depending on your preference, quietly play your favourite sleep sound on your phone
  • Close your eyes and focus on your breathing
  • It is important to regulate your breathing so that it is even
  • If you find yourself getting distracted, focus on your breathing again

Don't Let The Bed Bugs Bite!

It is important to feel comfortable and in the right state of mind. Sometimes it's hard to go to sleep right away. However, using the tips mentioned above, sleeping should come a lot easier. Sweet Dreams!
